# The Evolution of Kansas City Chiefs' Offensive Line in the Patrick Mahomes Era

Since Patrick Mahomes burst onto the NFL scene in 2018, the Kansas City Chiefs have been synonymous with high-octane offense. But behind Mahomes' wizardry—those no-look passes and improvisational scrambles—lies a crucial element often overlooked: the offensive line. In Topic 5 of our Chiefs Chronicle series, we dive deep into how this unit has evolved to become one of the league's most resilient and effective groups.

## Early Challenges: Building Around a Prodigy

When Mahomes took over as the starting quarterback, the Chiefs' offensive line was solid but not elite. Veterans like Mitchell Schwartz and Laurent Duvernay-Tardif provided stability, but the line faced tough tests against pass-rush heavyweights like the Baltimore Ravens and Pittsburgh Steelers. In 2018, Mahomes was sacked 34 times—a respectable number for a young QB, but it highlighted vulnerabilities.

Head coach Andy Reid and GM Brett Veach recognized the need for reinforcement. They prioritized athleticism and versatility, drafting players who could handle both run blocking and pass protection in their wide-zone scheme.

## Key Acquisitions and Draft Gems

The turning point came with strategic moves. In 2019, the Chiefs signed veteran tackle Eric Fisher to anchor the left side, while developing young talent like Orlando Brown Jr., acquired via trade in 2021. But the real game-changers were the draft picks:

- **Creed Humphrey (2021, Round 2)**: A center with All-Pro potential, Humphrey's intelligence and power have been pivotal. His snapping accuracy ensures Mahomes gets clean releases.
- **Trey Smith (2021, Round 6)**: The undrafted gem turned starter at guard, Smith's tenacity embodies the Chiefs' 'grit' ethos.
- **Jawaan Taylor (2023 Free Agency)**: A massive right tackle signing to replace Orlando Brown, Taylor brings Pro Bowl experience despite early adjustments.

These additions have reduced Mahomes' sack total to under 30 in recent seasons, giving him the pocket presence to orchestrate comebacks.

## Impact on Team Success

The offensive line's evolution isn't just about protection; it's about enabling the run game too. With Isiah Pacheco emerging as a dynamic back, the line's blocking has opened lanes for explosive plays. In Super Bowl LVII, they paved the way for a balanced attack that overwhelmed the Eagles.

Stats tell the story: The Chiefs rank top-5 in pass-blocking efficiency and have allowed the fewest pressures in the AFC West over the last three years (per Pro Football Focus). This stability lets coordinators like Matt Nagy scheme aggressively without fear.

## Looking Ahead: Sustainability and Challenges

As the Chiefs chase a three-peat in 2024, questions linger. Can aging vets like Joe Thuney maintain form? Will rookies like Kingsley Suamataia step up at left tackle? With a revamped receiving corps around Mahomes, the line's role remains paramount.
